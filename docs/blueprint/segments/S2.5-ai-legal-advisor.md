## Why this is one buildable unit
The Advisor page, its RAG-grounded streamed API, intent detection/handlers, and chat persistence are one conversational unit: a lawyer asks a question, gets a grounded, citation-safe answer with the turn saved — verifiable end-to-end in one pass (ask → grounded streamed reply → reload session and see history).

## Acceptance (definition of done for the segment)
- [ ] `/ai-advisor` gives conversational, citation-safe guidance grounded (RAG) in judgments (S2.1–S2.3) and statutes (S2.4).
- [ ] `/api/ai/advisor` streams responses and grounds each substantive claim; it refuses (or hedges) when it cannot ground an answer.
- [ ] Behaviour is tier-aware (lawyer vs student) and ChatGPT-style: no forced template or judgment list every turn — judgments only on a fresh/asked question.
- [ ] `intent-detection.ts` / `intent-handlers.ts` route each turn to the right behaviour; chat persists via `ChatSession`/`ChatMessage`.
- [ ] Session CRUD works: create/list sessions, read/rename/delete a session, append and read its messages, scoped to the authenticated user.
- [ ] Test expectation: integration tests cover grounded-answer with citations, refusal on ungroundable query, tier-aware tone, and session/message persistence + user-scoping.
- [ ] NFR checks: citation accuracy > 90%; hallucination < 10%; refuses when it cannot ground; sessions user-scoped.
- [ ] Verification: `POST /api/ai/advisor` with a legal question → streamed grounded answer citing real corpus authorities; the turn appears under `GET /api/chat/sessions/[id]/messages`.
