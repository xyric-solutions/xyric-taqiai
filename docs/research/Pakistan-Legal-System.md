# Pakistan Legal System Reference

## Overview

Pakistan's legal system is a hybrid of **British common law**, **Islamic law (Sharia)**, and **local statutory law**. The British colonial legacy provides the procedural and structural framework, while Islamic law influences criminal penalties, family law, and certain evidentiary rules.

---

## Constitutional Framework

### Constitution of Pakistan 1973

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Supreme law** | Constitution overrides all other laws |
| **Fundamental rights** | Articles 8-28 (right to life, liberty, fair trial, equality, religion) |
| **Judicial review** | Courts can strike down laws violating fundamental rights |
| **Article 10-A** | Right to fair trial and due process (added by 18th Amendment) |
| **Article 25** | Equality before law — no discrimination |
| **Article 199** | High Court writ jurisdiction |
| **Article 184(3)** | Supreme Court original jurisdiction for fundamental rights |


## Key Statutes

### Criminal Law

| Statute | Year | Purpose | Key Sections |
|---------|------|---------|--------------|
| **Pakistan Penal Code (PPC)** | 1860 | Defines criminal offenses | 302 (murder), 376 (rape), 420 (fraud), 489-F (dishonoured cheque) |
| **Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC)** | 1898 | Criminal court procedure | 154 (FIR), 173 (challan), 497 (bail), 498 (pre-arrest bail), 561-A (inherent powers) |
| **Qanun-e-Shahadat Order** | 1984 | Law of evidence | Replaces Indian Evidence Act; governs admissibility, witnesses, burden of proof |
| **Anti-Terrorism Act** | 1997 | Terrorism offenses | Separate courts, enhanced penalties |
| **Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act (PECA)** | 2016 | Cybercrime | Online harassment, data theft, electronic fraud |
| **Control of Narcotics Substances Act** | 1997 | Drug offenses | Trafficking, possession, sentencing |
| **Qisas & Diyat Ordinance** | 1990 | Islamic criminal penalties | Retribution (qisas) and compensation (diyat) for murder/hurt |

### Civil Law

| Statute | Year | Purpose | Key Provisions |
|---------|------|---------|----------------|
| **Code of Civil Procedure (CPC)** | 1908 | Civil court procedure | Order VII (plaint), Order VIII (written statement), Order XXXIX (injunction) |
| **Contract Act** | 1872 | Contract law | Formation, breach, remedies, specific performance |
| **Transfer of Property Act** | 1882 | Property transactions | Sale, mortgage, lease, gift, exchange |
| **Specific Relief Act** | 1877 | Equitable remedies | Specific performance, injunctions, declaratory decrees |
| **Limitation Act** | 1908 | Time limits for filing cases | Prescribes limitation periods for all civil actions |
| **Registration Act** | 1908 | Document registration | Requirements for registering property documents |

### Family Law

| Statute | Year | Purpose |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Muslim Family Laws Ordinance** | 1961 | Marriage, divorce, maintenance, inheritance for Muslims |
| **Family Courts Act** | 1964 | Jurisdiction and procedure for family disputes |
| **Guardian and Wards Act** | 1890 | Child custody and guardianship |
| **West Pakistan Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act** | 1962 | Application of Islamic personal law |

## Criminal Case Lifecycle

```
Offense → FIR (Section 154 CrPC)
    → Investigation (Police)
        → Challan (Section 173 CrPC) OR Cancellation Report
            → Charge Framing
                → Prosecution Evidence
                    → Defense Evidence
                        → Final Arguments
                            → Judgment (Conviction/Acquittal)
                                → Appeal (High Court)
                                    → Appeal (Supreme Court)
```

### Bail Framework

| Type | Section | When Filed | Key Test |
|------|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Pre-arrest Bail** | CrPC 498 | Before arrest | Reasonable grounds to believe arrest likely; no flight risk |
| **Post-arrest Bail** | CrPC 497 | After arrest | Further inquiry required; reasonable grounds; no likelihood of absconding |
| **Bail in Bailable Offenses** | CrPC 496 | After arrest | Right to bail — not discretionary |
| **Bail in Non-Bailable** | CrPC 497 | After arrest | Discretionary — court weighs evidence, gravity, flight risk |

## Civil Case Lifecycle

```
Cause of Action → Plaint (Order VII CPC)
    → Summons to Defendant
        → Written Statement (Order VIII CPC)
            → Framing of Issues
                → Evidence (Plaintiff → Defendant)
                    → Final Arguments
                        → Judgment & Decree
                            → Execution (Order XXI CPC)
                            → Appeal (Appellate Court)
```

## Precedent Authority

| Source | Binding? | On Whom |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **Supreme Court** | Binding | All courts in Pakistan |
| **Federal Shariat Court** | Binding | On Islamic law matters |
| **High Court** | Binding | District/subordinate courts within province |
| **High Court (other province)** | Persuasive | Other High Courts may follow but not bound |
| **Foreign courts** | Persuasive only | May be cited but not binding |

## Key Legal Principles

| Principle | Description | Application |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Benefit of doubt** | Accused entitled to benefit of every reasonable doubt | Criminal cases — prosecution must prove beyond reasonable doubt |
| **Res judicata** | Matter already decided cannot be re-litigated | Civil cases — prevents duplicate suits |
| **Stare decisis** | Follow established precedent | Courts bound by higher court decisions |
| **Audi alteram partem** | Hear both sides | Fundamental principle of natural justice |
| **Nemo judex in causa sua** | No one should be judge in their own case | Judicial impartiality |
| **Last seen evidence** | Last person seen with victim bears heavier burden | Criminal cases — circumstantial evidence |
| **Tazkiyah-al-Shuhood** | Testing credibility of witnesses | Evidence law — Islamic evidentiary principle |
